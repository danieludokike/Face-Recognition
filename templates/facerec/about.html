Description

Over the years, locating criminals has proven to be a difficult task. Previously, the approach was based on leads obtained from evidence found at the scene of the crime. Genetic evidence is relatively easy to locate, but criminals have become more adept at hiding their tracks and leaving no trace. As a result, facial recognition and surveillance technologies are now being used. Ultimately, face recognition technology has become an increasingly popular area of research due to its potential to enhance security and surveillance systems. The face is an essential element of social identity, and each face has distinctive features. Facial recognition is a unique biometric approach that is highly accurate and minimally invasive for crime detection. It involves naturally recognizing and verifying a person's identity from video sequences or photos using their face. In particular, the use of face recognition technology for crime detection has gained significant attention in recent years. With the increasing use of video surveillance cameras in public places, the ability to accurately and efficiently identify suspects through facial recognition can significantly improve the effectiveness of law enforcement and contribute to public safety. Identifying criminals is the most critical responsibility for law enforcement agencies, particularly in the context of the challenging and time-consuming task of locating criminals, who may be anywhere. In urban or densely populated areas, this task becomes even more daunting. In certain circumstances, the manual identification process may provide additional information regarding criminals. In this regard, this paper proposes an automatic face recognition system for crime detection that utilizes facial detection techniques to enable police officers to identify and apprehend criminals in public places. The facial recognition system described in this article is a 2 combination of the best facial recognition, classification, and feature extraction algorithms currently available. Automated facial recognition is a computerized technique that extracts significant facial features, such as the distance between the eyes, the width of the nose or jawline, and the eye color, among others. These characteristics are useful in categorization and record matching. This system involves two crucial processes: detection and recognition. The facial recognition process involves two primary phases: training and assessment. The algorithm is fed a sample of images and trained on the training set. The facial recognition assessment phase then compares the newly obtained test image to the database. In detection we need just to determine if there is some face in the image, but in recognition we want to determine whose face it is. (Tayyaba Zamindar et al., 2022) The face plays an important role in human communication, revealing unique character traits, emotions, and age, and allowing for social interaction. In recent years, biometric-based technologies, including facial recognition, have emerged as the most effective and reliable method for recognizing faces. Biometrics is a field that studies a person's inherent, specific characteristics, which can be beneficial in identifying an individual. Although individuals can deny the truth, biometric technologies such as fingerprints, faces, and eyes are relatively more reliable and difficult to forge since "the body never lies," and altering biometric features is incredibly challenging.
